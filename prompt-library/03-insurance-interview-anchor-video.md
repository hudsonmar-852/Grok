# Insurance Interview Anchor Video Prompt

Purpose: generate a stable professional insurance interview video anchor frame with slow camera drift for selecting the best face angle.

```text
[TOP PRIORITY — IDENTITY & REALISM]
Same person identity preserved throughout the entire clip.
Exact facial identity, bone structure, proportions, asymmetry, and apparent age remain unchanged.
No face swap, no reshaping, no beautification, no idealization.
Photographic realism is the highest priority.
The result must look like a real interview video captured in daily professional life.

[SILENT STATE — ABSOLUTE]
The character does NOT speak at any moment.
No lip movement for speech.
Mouth remains closed or gently relaxed.
This is a silent interview pause, not a talking segment.

[SCENE — INSURANCE INTERVIEW CONTEXT]
A real insurance interview setting in a modern professional office.
The subject is an insurance professional quietly listening or pausing between thoughts.
This is not a presentation, not a performance, not a promotional shoot.
The moment feels candid, credible, and unstaged.

[WARDROBE & PRESENCE]
Understated professional business attire in neutral tones.
Clothing fits naturally and practically.
No visible logos, text, or branding.
Overall presence feels calm, trustworthy, friendly, and approachable.

[CAMERA & FRAMING — GLOBAL RULE]
Single continuous shot.
One camera system.
No cuts.
No jump transitions.
Camera movement is extremely slow, smooth, and physically plausible.
Full-frame camera behavior.
Natural depth of field.
The clip feels like a paused frame extended in time.

[CAMERA ANGLE SEQUENCE — HORIZONTAL DRIFT ONLY]
The camera performs a very slow horizontal movement from right to left or left to right around the subject, without changing distance or height, as if gently sliding on a track.

START POSITION — Variant A
Eye-level medium close-up.
Framing from mid-chest to head.
Camera slightly to the right of the subject.
Subject looks slightly left of the camera toward the interviewer.

END POSITION — Variant B
Camera reaches a gentle three-quarter angle from the subject’s left side.
Camera height remains eye-level or slightly above.
Subject’s head adjusts minimally and naturally to maintain comfort.

No vertical camera movement.
No zoom.
No rotation spikes.
Only a slow, steady horizontal drift.

[MOTION — EXTREMELY SUBTLE & FRIENDLY]
Very slow, minimal head movement only.
Gentle micro head tilt or tiny facial adjustment.
Motion driven by breathing and comfort.
No nodding.
No emphasis gestures.
No posing.
Movement remains soft, calm, friendly, and photogenic.

[EXPRESSION — FRIENDLY & CAMERA-SAFE]
Soft, friendly, natural smile.
Relaxed and approachable.
No exaggerated smile.
No laughter.
No acting.
Expression remains stable so any frame can be used as a still image.

[EYES & GAZE]
Eyes appear clear, warm, and attentive.
Gaze remains slightly off-camera toward the interviewer.
No blank stare.
No direct address to the viewer.

[LIGHTING]
Soft, realistic office lighting.
Physically explainable sources such as window light or overhead office lights.
Natural shadow falloff.
No rim light.
No dramatic contrast.
No cinematic lighting effects.

[BACKGROUND]
Real office environment: meeting room, desk area, glass partition, or urban office interior.
Background remains passive and believable.
No studio backdrop.
No abstract background.

[OUTPUT]
One continuous video clip.
Ultra-realistic.
Silent.
No text, no logos, no borders, no overlays.
No explanation.
```
